
# A Region in Retreat: Press Freedom Across the Americas

## The 2026 World Press Freedom Index published by Reporters Without Borders (RSF) shows that press freedom across the Americas continues to decline, with more than half of the region's countries losing ground over the past year. This project analyzes the region's overall performance, identifies where press freedom has deteriorated most significantly, and examines the indicators contributing to the decline using data from RSF's 2026 index.

---

## Short description of what you aimed to accomplish

Through this data analysis, I aimed to examine press freedom across the Americas, a region that Reporters Without Borders identified as one of particular concern in its 2026 World Press Freedom Index. My analysis focused on understanding the extent of the region's decline by addressing the following questions:

1. How do the Americas compare with other RSF regions in overall press freedom scores in 2026?
2. How have press freedom scores changed across countries in the Americas since 2025?
3. Which countries have the strongest and weakest overall press freedom scores, and how do they influence the region's overall standing?
4. Which of RSF's five press freedom indicators are contributing most to the region's declining scores?

---

## Short description of your findings

I found that although the Americas rank second globally in average press freedom scores, behind only Europe/Balkans, the region trails Europe by a substantial margin and is much closer to the lower-ranked regions than its position initially suggests. More than half of the countries in the Americas experienced declining press freedom scores between 2025 and 2026, with South America showing the greatest concentration of declines.

The analysis also found that **economic** and **political** context were the weakest-performing indicators across the region, supporting RSF's concerns that financial instability and political pressure are major contributors to weakening press freedom. While Canada remains the region's strongest performer, the United States continues to fall further from its historical reputation as a leader in press freedom.
